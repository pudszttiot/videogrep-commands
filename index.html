Videogrep Commands

Home
videogrep --help
Displays all available commands and their usage options for videogrep.
videogrep --input example.mp4 --transcribe
Transcribes the audio from example.mp4, converting speech to text.
videogrep --input example.mp4 --search "hello" --search-type fragment
Searches for the word "hello" in example.mp4, extracting fragments containing the keyword. Example: Extracts short pieces where the word "hello" appears.
videogrep --input example.mp4 --search "hello" --search-type sentence
Searches for the word "hello" in example.mp4, extracting full sentences that contain it. Example: Finds full sentences in which "hello" is spoken, regardless of fragment length.
videogrep --input example.mp4 --search "hello" --search-type mash
Creates a mashup of fragments or sentences that contain the keyword "hello" from example.mp4. Example: Generates a creative mashup of various clips where "hello" is spoken.
videogrep --input example.mp4 --ngrams 1
Prints out in the terminal the most common single words
videogrep --input example.mp4 --ngrams 1 > results.txt
Prints out in the terminal the most common single words and saves the results of that search to results.txt
videogrep --input example.mp4 --ngrams 2
Prints out in the terminal the most common two word phrases
videogrep --search "hello" --json results.json
Searches for "hello" and saves the search results in results.json, including metadata like timestamps. Example: Ideal for saving clips with additional info for later processing.
videogrep --input example.mp4 --search "hello" --randomize
Searches for "hello" and randomizes the resulting clips. Example: The clips appear in a randomized order instead of the sequence they occur in the video.
videogrep -i example.mp4 -o clips.mp4 --search "hello" --keep 0.5
Extracts clips containing "hello" from example.mp4 while keeping 0.5 seconds before and after each occurrence. Example: Keeps context around the keyword and saves as clips.mp4.
videogrep --export transcriptions.csv
Exports the transcriptions of a video into a CSV file. Example: Saves all transcribed speech from a video into a CSV format.
videogrep -i example.mp4 -o clips.mp4 --search "hello" --max-splits 5
Extracts clips containing "hello" from example.mp4, allowing a maximum of 5 splits, and saves them as clips.mp4.
videogrep -i example.mp4 -o clips.mp4 --srt example.srt --search "keyword1 keyword2"
Extracts clips matching "keyword1" and "keyword2" from example.mp4, using subtitles from example.srt, and saves them as clips.mp4.
videogrep -i input_video.mp4 -o output_video.mp4 --search "keyword1 keyword2" [options]
Extracts clips based on keywords and specified options from input_video.mp4, saving them to output_video.mp4.
videogrep -i example.mp4 -o hello_clips.mp4 --search "hello"
Extracts clips containing "hello" from example.mp4 and saves them as hello_clips.mp4.
videogrep -i conversation.mp4 -o greetings.mp4 --search "hello" "goodbye"
Extracts clips with both "hello" and "goodbye" from conversation.mp4 and saves them as greetings.mp4.
videogrep -i lecture.mp4 -o keywords.mp4 --srt lecture.srt --search "data science" "machine learning"
Extracts clips containing "data science" and "machine learning" from lecture.mp4, using subtitles from lecture.srt, and saves them as keywords.mp4.
videogrep -i "Tucker Carlson Tonight - October 5th 2022.mp4" -o russia_clips.mp4 --search "russia" --randomize
Extracts clips containing "russia" from the specified video and saves them as russia_clips.mp4, randomizing their order.
